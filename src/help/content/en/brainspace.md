# BrainSpace

A canvas for thinking — capture loose notes, ideas, and fragments before they
become prose. Every space has one.

## At a glance

- **Add a note** from the toolbar; open it in the **detail drawer** to edit.
- Edit a note's **title** or **body** inline, right on the canvas.
- Give notes a **kind** to tell ideas, questions, and sources apart.
- Draw **connections** between notes, and **link a note to a document**.
- Attach up to **two images** per note.

## Notes

Add notes freely onto the canvas. A note can hold a quick thought, a quotation,
or a task. Click a note's title or body to **edit inline**, or open the
**detail drawer** for the full view. Right-click a note for its context menu;
**Escape** dismisses it. The sidebar reflects your **unsorted-notes count**.

## Note kinds

Notes come in different **kinds** so you can tell an idea from a question from a
source at a glance — a light structure on an otherwise free canvas.

## Connections

Draw **connections** between notes to map how ideas relate. The web of
connections often reveals the shape of an argument before you've written a word.

## Images

Attach images to a note from the detail drawer (up to **two per note**). They
persist locally across reloads and can be removed any time.

## Working alongside your draft

Use [Split view](views-and-modes#split) to keep BrainSpace beside your document
and pull captured ideas into the draft as you write.
