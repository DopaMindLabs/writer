# Annotations & highlights

Mark up your text with highlights and annotations to flag passages, leave notes
to yourself, and track what needs work.

## Highlighting

Select text and apply a highlight. Highlights use a small **palette** of colors
so you can build your own meaning — for example, yellow for "revisit", pink for
"strong line", and so on.

## Choosing a palette

The highlight palette is consistent across your writing, and it respects the
current theme, so highlights stay legible in light, dark, and high-contrast
modes alike. Adjust theme and appearance in
[Customization & settings](customization-and-settings).

## Annotations

Attach a note to a passage when a highlight alone is not enough. Annotations
keep your editorial thinking next to the text it refers to, without cluttering
the prose itself.

## Tips

- Decide what each color means before you start, and keep it consistent.
- Use highlights as a revision checklist: clear them as you resolve each one.
- Pair annotations with [BrainSpace](brainspace) to promote a note into a fuller
  idea.
