# Annotations & highlights

Mark up your text to flag passages, leave notes to yourself, and track what
needs work.

## At a glance

- **Select text** and apply a **highlight** from the colour palette.
- Use colours as a code — e.g. yellow "revisit", pink "strong line".
- Highlights adapt to your theme, staying legible everywhere.

## Highlighting

Select text and apply a highlight. Highlights use a small **palette** of colours
so you can build your own meaning.

## Choosing a palette

The highlight palette is consistent across your writing and **respects the
current theme**, so highlights stay legible in light, dark, and high-contrast
modes alike. Adjust theme and appearance in
[Customisation & settings](customization-and-settings).

## Annotations

Attach a note to a passage when a highlight alone isn't enough. Annotations keep
your editorial thinking next to the text it refers to, without cluttering the
prose itself.

## Tips

- Decide what each colour means before you start, and keep it consistent.
- Use highlights as a revision checklist — clear them as you resolve each one.
- Promote a note into a fuller idea in [BrainSpace](brainspace).
